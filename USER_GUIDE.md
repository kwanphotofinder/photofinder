# STUDENT GUIDE

## What Students CAN Do
✅ Search for photos from university events  
✅ Download photos of themselves  
✅ Save favorite photos  
✅ Request removal/privacy protection on specific photos  
✅ Export personal data  
✅ Delete all personal data (reference face + saved photos)

## What Students MUST Do
🔴 **Grant PDPA Consent** - Required before accessing photo search  
🔴 **Upload Reference Selfie** - Required to match photos  
🔴 **Have a valid Google account** - Required for login  

---

## Student Scenario 1: "I want to find photos of myself from an event"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Login to PhotoFinder**
   - Go to the PhotoFinder website
   - Click "Sign in with Google"
   - Use your MFU Google account (@student.mfu.ac.th or @mfu.ac.th)

2. **Grant PDPA Consent** (First-time only)
   - You'll see a consent page after login
   - Read the PDPA consent information carefully
   - Check both consent checkboxes
   - Click "Accept & Continue"
   - ℹ️ This means: You allow MFU to store your face data for photo matching and search purposes

3. **Upload Your Reference Selfie**
   - Go to **Dashboard**
   - On the left side, you'll see a large area with "No Image Set" placeholder
   - Click on this area to select your selfie file
   - Take a clear, well-lit selfie with your face fully visible
   - Face should occupy ~70% of the image (close-up)
   - ✅ GOOD: Direct face-forward selfie in good lighting
   - ❌ AVOID: Group photos, side angles, dark lighting, sunglasses on
   - Select your selfie file (JPG, PNG, WebP, HEIC)
   - Wait 10-30 seconds for the AI to process
   - You'll see a green checkmark (✓) appear when your face is saved

4. **Search for Photos**
   - Go to **Dashboard**
   - You'll see a "Find My Photos" section
   - The system automatically searches ALL events
   - Wait 15-60 seconds for results (depends on database size)
   - Results show photos that match your reference selfie
   - Each photo shows: Event name, date, photographer name

5. **Download Photos**
   - Click on any photo in the results
   - A modal will open showing the photo
   - Click "Download" button
   - Photo downloads as a high-quality JPEG with MFU PhotoFinder watermark
   - ℹ️ Watermark is added to verify authenticity

6. **Save Favorite Photos**
   - While viewing a photo, click the "Save" or heart icon
   - Photo is added to your **Favorites** page
   - Go to **Favorites** anytime to see all saved photos
   - Click "Remove from Saved" to delete from favorites

---

## Student Scenario 2: "I found a photo I don't want to see"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Request Photo Removal**
   - While viewing the unwanted photo, click "Request Removal"
   - Choose your request type:
     - **DELETION** - Delete the photo completely from the system
     - **TAKEDOWN** - Remove the photo from public view (admins only)
     - **PRIVACY** - Blur/pixelate your face in the photo

2. **Provide Reason**
   - Type a reason if desired (optional but recommended)
   - Example: "I look bad in this photo", "This is too blurry"

3. **Submit Request**
   - Click "Submit"
   - You'll see: "Removal request submitted successfully!"
   - An admin will review within 24-48 hours
   - You'll receive a notification when the request is processed

---

## Student Scenario 3: "I want to manage my privacy"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Go to Privacy & Consent Settings**
   - Click your profile icon (top right)
   - Go to **Settings** → Click the **"Privacy & Consent"** tab

2. **View Your Consent Status**
   - You'll see a status indicator showing "Opted In" (green) or "Opted Out" (gray)
   - This shows whether face search is currently active

3. **Export Your Data**
   - Scroll to the bottom of the Privacy & Consent tab
   - Click the **"Download Data"** or **"Export Data"** button
   - A JSON file downloads with:
     - Your profile information
     - All photos you've downloaded
     - Your saved photos list
     - Your removal requests
   - ℹ️ This is your right under PDPA law

4. **Delete All Your Data**
   - Click the **"Delete My Data"** button
   - ⚠️ WARNING: This action is PERMANENT
   - Your reference face embedding will be deleted
   - All your saved photos will be removed
   - You will NOT be able to search for photos anymore
   - A confirmation dialog will appear - read carefully and confirm
   - Processing takes 5-10 minutes

5. **Reset Your Reference Selfie** (without deleting all data)
   - If you just want to replace your selfie (not delete everything):
   - Go back to **Dashboard**
   - Click the **"Reset Profile"** button below your current selfie
   - Then upload a new selfie to replace it

## What Photographers CAN Do
✅ Upload photos from events  
✅ View photo analytics (views, downloads, shares)  
✅ See which students found photos of themselves  
✅ Download a list of all uploaded photos  
✅ Tag photos by event  
✅ Export personal data  
✅ Delete all personal data  

## What Photographers MUST Do
🔴 **Have a verified photographer account** - Request from admin  
🔴 **Get event permission** - Can only upload to events admin approved  
🔴 **Sign PDPA data processing agreement** - Required by MFU  
🔴 **Upload high-quality photos** - Minimum resolution: 1024x768px  

---

## Photographer Scenario 1: "I want to upload photos from the sports event"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Login as Photographer**
   - Use your MFU Google account
   - Your role should be "Photographer" (set by admin)
   - If not, contact admin: kwanphotofinder@gmail.com

2. **Go to Photographer Page**
   - After login, click your profile icon → You'll be redirected to **/photographer**
   - You see three workspace tabs at the top:
     - **Upload Workflow** (for uploading photos) - START HERE
     - **Analytics** (to view engagement stats)
     - **Manage Photos** (to review uploaded photos)

3. **Make Sure You're in Upload Workflow Tab**
   - Click the **"Upload Workflow"** tab if it's not already selected
   - You'll see three steps:
     - Step 1: Choose Event
     - Step 2: Add Files  
     - Step 3: Upload

4. **Select the Event (Step 1)**
   - Click the dropdown: **"-- Select an event --"**
   - Choose the event (e.g., "Sports Day 2026-05-01")
   - ℹ️ Only shows events approved for you by admins
   - If your event isn't listed, request access from admin
   - The dropdown updates to show: "Files will be uploaded to [Event Name]."

5. **Add Files (Step 2)**
   - Below the event selector, there's a large drag-and-drop area
   - Click **"Select files"** button or drag photos directly into the area
   - Select multiple JPG/PNG/WebP files at once
   - You can select up to 50 photos at once
   - Maximum file size: 15MB per photo
   - Selected files appear in a list below

6. **Review Your Queue**
   - You'll see cards showing:
     - Events: Available events to upload to
     - Selected: Number of files ready
     - If any files have errors, they appear in red
   - Click **"Clear All"** if you need to remove all files and start over

7. **Start Upload (Step 3)**
   - Click the **"Upload [X] Photos"** button (where X = number of files)
   - Upload progress shows: "Uploading 3/50 completed"
   - Each photo shows:
     - ✅ Success - Photo uploaded and processed
     - ⏳ Processing - AI extracting faces
     - ❌ Failed - Check the error message in red
   
   - Common errors:
     - "No faces detected" - Try a clearer photo
     - "Multiple faces detected" - Edit to show only one clear face
     - "File too large" - Reduce file size (max 15MB)

8. **Completion**
   - All photos processed within 30-60 seconds
   - Progress bar reaches 100%
   - Queue automatically clears after upload
   - Photos are now searchable by students

## Photographer Scenario 2: "I want to see who downloaded my photos"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Go to Analytics Tab**
   - Click your profile icon → Go to **/photographer** page
   - Click the **"Analytics"** tab at the top

2. **View Overall Statistics**
   - At the top, you see four stat cards:
     - **Total Events**: How many events you've covered
     - **Total Photos**: Total photos you've uploaded
     - **Total Views**: How many times your photos were viewed
     - **Total Downloads**: How many times students downloaded your photos

3. **View Trend Chart**
   - The chart shows:
     - **X-axis**: Date (last 7-30 days, depending on filter)
     - **Y-axis**: Number of views and downloads
     - Blue area = views, green area = downloads
   - Use the dropdown to change trend days: "Last 7 days", "Last 14 days", etc.

4. **View Per-Event Analytics Table**
   - Scroll down to see "Event Matrix"
   - Table shows for each event:
     - Event name and date
     - Number of photos you uploaded
     - Views this event received
     - Downloads from this event
     - Engagement rate (% of views that led to downloads)
   - Events with highest engagement appear first

5. **Identify Best Performing Events**
   - Sort by "Engagement Rate" to see which events resonate most
   - Use this to decide which events need more uploads
   - "Peak Views" and "Peak Downloads" show the highest single-day stats

## Photographer Scenario 3: "A student says I uploaded a bad photo of them"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Understand Student Rights**
   - Students can request removal of any photo
   - They can choose:
     - **DELETION** - Photo deleted permanently
     - **PRIVACY** - Your face is blurred
     - **TAKEDOWN** - Photo removed from public view

2. **Respond to Removal Request**
   - When a removal request is submitted, admin reviews it
   - You'll be notified if your photo is affected
   - Admin decides based on:
     - Student privacy rights (PDPA law)
     - Photo quality/appropriateness
     - University policies

3. **Best Practices**
   - Upload high-quality, clear photos
   - Avoid unflattering angles or lighting
   - Include diverse subjects (try to capture everyone)
   - Get verbal permission if possible
   - Delete obvious bad photos before uploading

## Admin Scenario 1: "I want to create a new event"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Go to Admin Dashboard**
   - Login with admin account
   - Click your profile → **Admin Dashboard**

2. **Navigate to Events**
   - Click **"Events Management"** in sidebar
   - Click **"Create New Event"** button

3. **Fill Event Details**
   - **Event Name**: "Sports Day 2026" (required)
   - **Event Slug**: "sports-day-2026" (auto-generated, unique identifier)
   - **Event Date**: Select date and time (required)
   - **Location**: "MFU Sports Complex" (optional but recommended)
   - **Description**: "Annual university sports competition" (optional)

4. **Set Event Status**
   - **DRAFT**: Not visible to students yet
   - **PUBLISHED**: Students can search for photos
   - **ARCHIVED**: Old events, no new uploads

5. **Save Event**
   - Click **"Create Event"**
   - Event is created in DRAFT status
   - You can edit or delete it anytime

6. **Publish Event**
   - Once ready, click **"Publish"**
   - Event becomes searchable
   - Photographers can now upload photos to this event

## Admin Scenario 2: "A student requests removal of a photo"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **View Removal Requests**
   - Go to **Admin Dashboard** → **Removal Requests**
   - Shows all pending requests with:
     - Student name
     - Photographer name
     - Removal type (DELETE, TAKEDOWN, PRIVACY)
     - Reason provided
     - Date submitted

2. **Review the Photo**
   - Click on the request
   - Photo is displayed with full metadata
   - See student comment and reason

3. **Make Decision**
   - **APPROVE**: Agree to the removal request
     - If DELETION: Photo is permanently deleted from system
     - If PRIVACY: Face is blurred in the photo
     - If TAKEDOWN: Photo is hidden from public search
   
   - **REJECT**: Deny the removal request
     - Photo stays as-is
     - Student is notified of rejection
     - Include reason for rejection

4. **Document Decision**
   - Type reason for your decision (required)
   - Example: "Photo meets university standards, denial stands"

5. **Notify Student**
   - Click **"Send Notification"**
   - Student receives LINE/Email update
   - Includes decision and reason

## Admin Scenario 4: "Photos have low-confidence face detections"

### Step-by-Step Instructions:

1. **Review Low-Confidence Photos**
   - Go to **Admin Dashboard** → **Low-Confidence Photos**
   - Shows photos where AI detected faces but with <60% confidence
   - These might have:
     - Poor lighting
     - Blurry faces
     - Partial faces
     - Bad angles

2. **Review Each Photo**
   - Click photo to see full details
   - View: Confidence score, bounding boxes, timestamp

3. **Make Decision**
   - **Approve**: Mark as approved (dismisses from queue, photo stays searchable)
   - **Delete**: Remove photo permanently from system
   - **Go to Event Edit**: Edit the event to see more photos or adjust settings

4. **Preview Photo First**
   - Click **"Preview"** button to open the photo in full size
   - This helps you assess photo quality before deciding
   - You can see confidence scores and face detection overlays

5. **Review the Metrics**
   - Each photo shows:
     - **Min confidence**: Lowest confidence score for faces in photo
     - **Low-confidence faces**: Count of faces below your threshold
     - **Total faces**: All faces detected in the photo
   - Use these metrics to decide: Is this photo useful despite low confidence?

1. **Go to Analytics Dashboard**

   - **Database Health**: Connection status, disk usage

# COMMON ISSUES & TROUBLESHOOTING

## For All Users

### "I can't login"
**Possible Causes:**
- Wrong email or password
- Google account not connected to MFU
- Account not activated yet

**Solutions:**
1. Try again with correct email
2. Make sure using MFU Google account (@student.mfu.ac.th or @mfu.ac.th)
3. Contact admin if account not created yet

---

### "The page is loading slowly"
**Possible Causes:**
- AI service (Hugging Face) is waking up (free tier cold start)
- Network connection is slow
- Database is busy

**Solutions:**
1. Wait 2-3 minutes (AI service may be sleeping)
2. Try refreshing page (Ctrl+R)
3. Check your internet connection
4. Contact support if issue persists

---

## For Students

### "I uploaded a reference selfie but search isn't working"
**Possible Causes:**
- No faces detected in selfie
- Multiple faces detected (group photo)
- Face too small in image
- Poor lighting

**Solutions:**
1. Try new selfie: Close-up, well-lit, facing camera
2. Delete old reference, upload new one
3. Make sure alone in photo (no other people)
4. Avoid sunglasses or hats

---

### "No photos found matching my face"
**Possible Causes:**
- No photographers have uploaded to your events yet
- Face doesn't match (different appearance since selfie)
- Photos don't have clear face detections
- You haven't attended any events

**Solutions:**
1. Check which events you attended
2. Ask photographers if they've uploaded photos yet
3. Try new reference selfie if look different now
4. Wait for photographers to upload photos

---

### "I can't link my LINE account"
**Possible Causes:**
- Haven't added PhotoFinder LINE OA yet
- LINE account not connected to phone
- Browser doesn't allow popups

**Solutions:**
1. First add @042nimvi as LINE friend
2. Go back to PhotoFinder Settings → "Link LINE Account"
3. Check if browser has popup blocker enabled
4. Try on mobile phone if desktop not working

---

## For Photographers

### "Upload failed: 'No faces detected'"
**Possible Causes:**
- Image quality too low
- Faces are too far away in image
- Image is blurry
- Poor lighting

**Solutions:**
1. Check image in photo editor
2. Verify faces are clear and visible
3. Try different angle or lighting
4. Resize image if too small

---

### "Upload taking too long"
**Possible Causes:**
- Uploading many large files (>50 photos)
- AI service is processing slowly
- Network connection interrupted

**Solutions:**
1. Upload 10-20 photos at a time
2. Wait 2-3 minutes for AI service
3. Check internet connection
4. Try uploading again in few minutes

---

## For Admins

### "System shows 'Low Disk Space' warning"
**Possible Causes:**
- Too many photos stored
- Database logs not cleaned up
- Temporary files accumulating

**Solutions:**
1. Review and delete old archived events
2. Contact Neon support to upgrade tier
3. Archive events not needed anymore

---

### "Cannot approve removal request"
**Possible Causes:**
- Photo already deleted
- Request status changed
- Permission issue

**Solutions:**
1. Refresh page and try again
2. Check request status
3. Contact SUPER_ADMIN if permission denied
